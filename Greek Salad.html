Best Organic Recipes

Higher nutrient density

Higher nutrient density means more nutrients per calorie. Nutrients are vitamins, minerals, protein, fibre, and healthy fats. Nutrient-dense foods help us avoid excess calories and chronic diseases. Examples are fruits, vegetables and lean meats. They have antioxidants, phytochemicals, and other beneficial compounds.

Less harmful pesticides

Less harmful pesticides are those that pose less risk to human and environmental health than conventional pesticides. They may break down rapidly, target specific pests, or minimize exposure. Examples are biopesticides, insecticidal soapss, and oils. They can control insects, weeds, fungi, and other pests with fewer side effects.

Better taste

Better taste means more enjoyment and satisfaction from food. Taste depends on freshness, quality, and natural flavors of ingredients. Organic food may taste better than non-organic food, as it is grown without chemicals that can alter or reduce taste. Organic food also preserves the original aroma and texture of food.

Let’s explore the potential health benefits of a Delightful Greek salad (also known as “Horiatiki”).
This Mediterranean classic is not only delicious but also packed with nutrients. Here’s why you should consider adding Greek salad to your menu:

1) Rich in Antioxidants

Greek salad ingredients like tomatoes and olives are loaded with antioxidants. These compounds protect our cells from damage caused by free radicals, contributing to overall health and wellness.

2) Heart Health

The olive oil in Greek salad contains heart-friendly monounsaturated fats. These fats help lower bad cholesterol levels, reducing the risk of heart disease and artery blockages.

3) Bone Health

Feta cheese, a staple in Greek salads, provides a good dose of calcium. Calcium is essential for maintaining strong bones and teeth, especially as we age.

4) Hydration

Greek salad includes cucumbers, which are about 95% water. Staying hydrated is crucial for various bodily functions, and cucumbers help replenish fluids, especially in warm climates or after physical activity.

5) Weight Management

With its mix of fresh vegetables, Greek salad is naturally low in calories yet high in nutrients. The fiber content from ingredients like cucumbers and tomatoes promotes a feeling of fullness after eating.

6) Digestive Health

The insoluble fiber in Greek salad aids digestion and supports regular bowel movements.

7) Boosted Immunity

The variety of vegetables in Greek salad provides essential nutrients that support the immune system. Vitamins A and C play a role in immune function and overall health

8) Healthy Skin

Antioxidants and hydration from Greek salad contribute to skin health. Proper hydration and nutrient intake help maintain a radiant complexion

9) Anti-Inflammatory Properties

Olive oil and other ingredients in Greek salad have anti-inflammatory effects, benefiting overall well-being

11) Blood Pressure Regulation

The combination of heart-healthy fats, antioxidants, and nutrients in Greek salad may help regulate blood pressure

Ingredients

There are Nine Ingrediantes used to make Greek Salad

Tomatoes

Use Roma tomatoes or vine-ripe tomatoes. They stay somewhat firm when ripe, which pairs well with the crunchy bell pepper and onion.

Cucumber

Opt for English cucumbers as they tend to be sweeter. If you have standard slicing cucumbers, make sure to fully peel their bitter waxy skin.

Onion

Red onion is sweet and mild enough to serve raw. To further take the edge off, a quick soak in ice water and a dash of vinegar does the trick.

Olives

Briny Kalamata olives are a favorite for a traditional Greek salad, but any high-quality olive will work.

Bell Pepper

Choose a firm and smooth green bell pepper, which is the only bell pepper used in an authentic Greek salad.

Feta Cheese

Opt for whole blocks of feta packed in brine, as it not only boasts better flavor but also allows you to add large chunks of feta.

Extra Virgin Olive Oil

Use a full-flavor Greek olive oil, like our Private Reserve from Kyparissia.

Red Wine Vinegar

Adds a dash of acidity to create an easy vinaigrette with no whisking necessary

Seasoning

This salad is seasoned simply with a sprinkle of kosher salt and dried oreganos.

How to Make Greek Salad

Shock the Onion (Optional)

Halve and thinly slice one red onion into half moons. If you’d like to mellow its raw taste, fill a small bowl with ice water and soak the sliced onion for a few minutes.

Assemble the Salad

Combine the sliced tomatoes, cucumber, green bell pepper, and olives in a large serving bowl. Add the crumbled feta cheese.

Make the Vinaigrette

Whisk together minced garlic, dried oregano, Dijon mustard, red wine vinegar, kosher salt, and freshly ground black pepper. Slowly add the extra virgin olive oil to create an emulsion.

Dress the Salad

Pour the vinaigrette over the vegetables. Toss everything lightly to coat.

Serve Immediately

Enjoy your authentic Greek salad!
