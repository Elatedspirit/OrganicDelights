Best Organic Recipes

Higher nutrient density

Higher nutrient density means more nutrients per calorie. Nutrients are vitamins, minerals, protein, fibre, and healthy fats. Nutrient-dense foods help us avoid excess calories and chronic diseases. Examples are fruits, vegetables and lean meats. They have antioxidants, phytochemicals, and other beneficial compounds.

Less harmful pesticides

Less harmful pesticides are those that pose less risk to human and environmental health than conventional pesticides. They may break down rapidly, target specific pests, or minimize exposure. Examples are biopesticides, insecticidal soapss, and oils. They can control insects, weeds, fungi, and other pests with fewer side effects.

Better taste

Better taste means more enjoyment and satisfaction from food. Taste depends on freshness, quality, and natural flavors of ingredients. Organic food may taste better than non-organic food, as it is grown without chemicals that can alter or reduce taste. Organic food also preserves the original aroma and texture of food.

Dal Makhani

Dal Makhani is a classic North Indian dish made primarily from whole black lentils (urad dal) and red kidney beans (rajma). The lentils and beans are cooked until soft and then simmered in a rich and creamy sauce made with tomatoes, onions, ginger-garlic paste, and a blend of aromatic spices like cumin, turmeric, red chili powder, and garam masala. The dish gets its creamy texture from the addition of cream or cashew paste, making it indulgent and satisfying. Dal Makhani is typically served hot and garnished with fresh coriander leaves, and it pairs well with rice, naan, or roti. It's a comforting and flavorful dish enjoyed across India and around the world.

Shahi Paneer

Shahi Paneer, a hallmark of North Indian cuisine, epitomizes richness and flavor. This sumptuous dish stars paneer, Indian cottage cheese, bathed in a luscious sauce melded with aromatic spices and cream. The base comprises onions, tomatoes, ginger, and garlic, sautéed to golden perfection, then blended to a silky consistency. Cashews or almonds enrich the sauce, adding opulence. Delicately spiced with cumin, coriander, turmeric, and garam masala, it creates a symphony of taste. Garnished with nuts and fresh coriander, it captivates both eyes and palate. Shahi Paneer, a fixture in celebrations, reflects the grandeur of North Indian gastronomy, offering a culinary experience fit for royalty. Read More
